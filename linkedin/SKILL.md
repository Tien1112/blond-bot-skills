LinkedIn Post Skill

\# LinkedIn Post Rules

Practical rules for writing LinkedIn posts that grow followers. Distilled from Ruben Hassid's playbook (770K+ followers, \~1,000 new followers/day).

\## Core Principle

\*\*No one cares about you. Readers only care about themselves.\*\*

They read your post alone, facing a screen, with a hundred problems to deal with. If your post is about your achievement, they scroll past. If your post helps them solve their problem, they follow.

\- Personal posts make your mom proud. They don't make strangers follow you.
\- "We're proud to announce..." \= instant scroll.
\- "Here's how you can..." \= attention.

\*\*Test every post:\*\* Is this about ME or about the READER? If it's about you, rewrite it.


\## The Hook

The hook is the only thing that matters before the click. On LinkedIn, that's exactly 2 short sentences (\~55 characters each) before the "...more" button.

\*\*The hook's only job:\*\* break the reader's scrolling pattern and make them click "...more."

\### Hook Techniques

\| Technique | Example |
\|-----------|---------|
\| Contradiction | "The worst LinkedIn posts get the most followers." |
\| Specific number + unexpected context | "I unfollowed 2,000 people. My engagement tripled." |
\| Direct accusation | "You're writing LinkedIn posts for your mom, not your audience." |
\| Stolen thought | "You know your LinkedIn posts are boring. So does everyone scrolling past them." |
\| Absurd reframe | "Your LinkedIn hook has 1.2 seconds to live. Most die instantly." |

\### Hook Rules

\- Must be about the READER or a universal tension, never about you
\- Create an open loop: unanswered question, contradiction, or bold claim
\- No personal achievements in the hook
\- No emoji openers
\- No hashtags in the hook
\- Should feel like something a friend would text you that makes you reply "wait, explain"


\## Media

The image/video/PDF takes up \~80% of the screen. The hook takes \~20%. The rest: 0%.

\### What works

\- \*\*Best format:\*\* 1080 x 1350 single image
\- Scrappy, unbranded feel
\- Useful enough to bookmark or send to a colleague
\- Cheat sheets, infographics, simple graphs

\### What doesn't work

\- Walls of text with no media
\- Overproduced branded visuals (looks like an ad \= performs like an ad)
\- Videos (people are supposed to be working, not binge-watching)
\- Polished corporate graphics

\### AI Media Workflow

1\. Go to Pinterest, search "\[your niche] + infographic" or "cheat sheet"
2\. Download a reference image you like
3\. Upload to Gemini (Thinking model) with this prompt:

\`\`\`
Extract all of the information from this infographic so another designer could remake it.
Your goal is to brief it, as if the designer was Gemini nano-banana (reverse prompting).
\`\`\`

4\. Find a second reference image with a "handwritten style" or scrappy organic feel
5\. Follow up in Gemini:

\`\`\`
Now remake this infographic with the information, but with this style
I just uploaded (copy only the style, your content comes from what you just shared).
\`\`\`


\## Post Structure Prompt

Use this prompt to draft a post that's about the reader, not about you:

\`\`\`
I want to find the best angle and topic for one LinkedIn post that turns
my expertise into something useful, relatable, and 100% about the reader
\- not me bragging - by asking me clarifying questions about what real
problem my audience is struggling with, what piece of my expertise would
feel most useful to a stranger, and how to frame it so the reader sees
themselves in it so that the post feels like the reader is reading about
themselves and gives them a clear reason to follow me.

First, read the uploaded files completely before responding.

DO NOT start executing yet. Instead, ask me clarifying questions so we
can refine the approach together step by step.

Only begin work once we've aligned.
\`\`\`


\## Hook Generation Prompt

After drafting a post, use this to generate hooks:

\`\`\`
Now let's write the hook for this post - the first 2 lines readers see
before they have to click "...more" on LinkedIn (roughly 2 short
sentences of \~55 characters max each).

The hook's only job is to break the reader's scrolling pattern and make
them NEED to click "...more." It should feel like a pattern interrupt -
something unexpected, counterintuitive, or so specific the reader
thinks "wait, what?"

Rules:
\- The hook must be about the READER or a universal tension, never about me.
\- It should create an open loop: unanswered question, contradiction,
&#x20; or a bold claim the reader can't ignore.
\- Avoid anything that sounds like a personal achievement, no emoji
&#x20; openers, no hashtags.
\- It should feel like something a friend would text you that makes
&#x20; you reply "wait, explain."

Hook techniques:
1\. Contradiction - say something that sounds wrong
2\. Specific number + unexpected context
3\. Direct accusation - call the reader out
4\. Stolen thought - say what the reader secretly thinks but won't say
5\. Absurd reframe - take something mundane and make it dramatic

Generate 10 hook options organized by technique. For each, write the
exact 2 lines as they would appear on LinkedIn.
\`\`\`


\## Consistency

\- Post every day. No exceptions.
\- 20 min to prepare a post, 30 min to comment on others
\- Action produces information: posting more gives you the data to post better
\- If you can't commit 30 minutes a day, you won't outpace people who do
\- Someone less talented than you is getting what you want by simply being consistent


\## Quick Checklist Before Posting

\- \[ ] Is this post about the READER, not about me?
\- \[ ] Does the hook break a scrolling pattern in 2 short lines?
\- \[ ] Is there a 1080x1350 image that's useful and scrappy (not ad-like)?
\- \[ ] Would the reader screenshot this or send it to a colleague?
\- \[ ] Does it solve one specific problem for one specific person?
\- \[ ] Did I remove all "we're proud" / "I'm excited" / "thrilled to announce" language?
